The root causes

Why crashes happen

Understanding the leading causes of road accidents is the first step toward preventing them. Most crashes share a common factor: human behaviour.

Speeding

Driving faster than the posted limit or conditions allow reduces reaction time and dramatically increases crash severity. A pedestrian hit at 65 km/h has a 85% chance of fatality.

→ Involved in ~31% of fatal crashes

Distracted Driving

Phone use behind the wheel makes you 4× more likely to crash. Taking your eyes off the road for just 5 seconds at 90 km/h means travelling 125 metres blind.

→ Causes 1 in 4 crashes

Drunk Driving

Alcohol impairs judgement, slows reaction time, and reduces coordination. At a blood alcohol level of 0.08%, crash risk is 4× higher than sober driving.

→ Claims over 10,000 lives in the US alone annually

Not Using Seatbelts

Seatbelts reduce the risk of death by up to 50% for front-seat occupants and 75% for rear-seat passengers. Yet millions still choose not to buckle up.

→ Seatbelts save 15,000 lives per year in the US

Running Red Lights

Intersection violations account for nearly 40% of all crashes in urban areas. Side-impact collisions from red-light running are among the most deadly crash types.

→ ~700 deaths per year from red-light running (US)

Driver Fatigue

Drowsy driving impairs performance similarly to drunk driving. After 20 hours without sleep, crash risk equals that of a blood-alcohol level of 0.08%.

→ Responsible for 20% of all serious crashes

What you can do

Simple habits that
save lives

Safe roads start with safe choices. Here are eight evidence-backed behaviours every road user should practise.

01

Always wear your seatbelt

Click it before you start the engine — every trip, every seat, every time. Ensure all passengers are buckled, including those in the back.

02

Put the phone away

If you need to use your phone, pull over safely. Even hands-free calls can divert your attention enough to miss a hazard.

03

Respect speed limits

Speed limits reflect the maximum safe speed in ideal conditions. Rain, darkness, or heavy traffic demand slower speeds.

04

Never drink and drive

Plan ahead — designate a driver, book a taxi, or use a rideshare app. There is no safe level of alcohol when behind the wheel.

05

Keep a safe following distance

Maintain at least a 3-second gap in normal conditions. Double it in rain or at night. Tailgating is a leading cause of rear-end crashes.

06

Rest before long journeys

Take a 15-minute break every 2 hours on long drives. If you feel drowsy, pull over safely — fatigue cannot be overcome by willpower alone.

07

Check your vehicle regularly

Tyre pressure, brakes, lights, and wiper blades should all be inspected monthly. A well-maintained vehicle responds better in emergencies.

08

Watch for vulnerable users

Cyclists, pedestrians, and motorcyclists are far more exposed than car occupants. Give them extra space, especially at intersections.
